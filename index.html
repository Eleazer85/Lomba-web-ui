Little things

bring

Big changes

Our existence means a lot to the environment. Doing small things does not mean that our existence is in vain. The small impact we make will definitely make a big difference to the environment.





What's the data tell us?


We can see from the graph data on the side, in 2020 the world specifically in Indonesia was 'resting', carbon emissions decreased by almost 10%. But when activities returned to normal, carbon emissions increased to 17-20% from 2020.

What about microplastics? According to a prediction made by one of the professors from IILM University, the prediction of microplastic emissions in 2030 will reach 1.2 million tons. So we can predict that from 2024 to 2030 the increase in emissions will approximately touch 50%.

Use Energy Wisely.

Be mindful of energy use—turn off lights when not needed, unplug devices, and invest in energy-efficient appliances. Every small effort adds up to a big impact.



Create Less Waste

Reduce, reuse, and recycle. By minimizing waste production, we conserve resources and cut down emissions from waste processing and landfill decomposition.



Minimize Food Waste.

Plan meals ahead, store food properly, and repurpose leftovers. By reducing food waste, we conserve resources like water and energy, and also reduce methane emissions from landfills.
