Annual CO2 Emissions

Source: ourworldindata.org

We can see from the graph data on the side, in 2020 the world specifically in Indonesia was 'resting', carbon emissions decreased by almost 10%. But when activities returned to normal, carbon emissions increased to 17-20% from 2020.

Microplastic Emissions In Oceans

Source: ourworldindata.org

What about microplastics? According to a prediction made by one of the professors from IILM University, the prediction of microplastic emissions in 2030 will reach 1.2 million tons. So we can predict that from 2024 to 2030 the increase in emissions will approximately touch 50%.

Global Primary Energy Consumption

Source: ourworldindata.org

The annual change in energy consumption in Indonesia, as seen in the chart, shows a steady increase of up to 10% from 2015 to 2018. During the pandemic era, there was a significant decrease of up to 15%. However, when conditions returned to normal, energy consumption experienced a sharp spike, increasing by 30%.

Global Temperature

Source: climate.nasa.gov

The graph shows global surface temperature changes from 1951 to 1980, with 2023 being the warmest year on record since 1880. NASA’s findings, consistent with NOAA and other research groups, reveal that Earth's temperature in 2023 was 2.45°F (1.36°C) higher than the late 19th-century average. The past 10 years have been the warmest on record.

Global Primary Energy Consumption

Source: ourworldindata.org

The demand for energy growing all across the world. This interactive chart shows how global energy consumption has been changing from year to year. The change is given as a percentage of consumption in the previous year. We see that global energy consumption has increased nearly every year, Global energy consumption continues to grow, but it does seem to be slowing averaging around 1% to 2% per year.
